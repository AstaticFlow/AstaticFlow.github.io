Elizabeth Jane Cochran

The immersive journalist and world traveller known to the world as “Nellie Bly”.


Check out some snippets of Nellie Bly’s life from beginning to end:

May 5, 1864 – Elizabeth Jane Cochran (later known as “Nellie Bly”) is born in Cochran’s Mills Pennsylvania, U.S.A.

1871 – Elizabeth’s father, Michael Cochran, dies while her family is living in Cochran’s Mills

1879 – Elizabeth becomes a student at Indiana Normal School. Financial hardship forces her to drop out of after her first term.

1880 – Elizabeth and her family move to Allegheny City, Pennsylvania. She writes a response to the Pittsburgh Dispatch in regards to an article suggesting women should only be housewives. George Madden, editor of the paper, meets Elizabeth. After having her write several stories, Madden hires her under the pseudonym “Nellie Bly”.

1880 to 1887 – Elizabeth works as an investigative reporter for the Pittsburgh Dispatch covering working conditions for women in factories. Due to local politics and complaints, she is moved to reporting on women’s interests. Bored with this, she leaves for Mexico and serves as a foreign correspondent in until having to leave the country under treat of arrest for the content of her stories.

1887 – Elizabeth leaves her position at the Pittsburgh Dispatch and moves to New York City where she accepted an undercover story for the New York World and faked mental illness to be admitted to the Blackwell Island Women’s Lunatic Asylum. After 10 days, the newspaper secures her release and Elizabeth publishes a series detailing the poor conditions at the asylum later known as Ten Days in a Mad-House.

1888 – Elizabeth proposes that the paper send her on a trip around the world to emulate the book Around the World in Eighty Days.

November 14, 1989 – Elizabeth departs aboard the steamer Augusta Victoria for her world trip.

1989 – Elizabeth travels mainly by rail and ship through Europe and Asia, meeting Jules Verne along the way. She sends reports back to the U.S.A. by telegraph and post.

January 21, 1890 – Elizabeth arrives in San Francisco on the RMS Oceanic where she then boards a chartered train.

January 25, 1890 - Elizabeth arrives in New Jersey at the end of her voyage. The trip takes 72 days in all after she arrives in New York. Elizabeth soon quits her job as a reporter and begins writing novels.

1893 – Elizabeth becomes a reporter once again and manages to secure an interview with Lizzie Halliday, an infamous serial killer.

1895 – Elizabeth marries the millionaire Robert Seaman. She quits her job as a journalist and takes over management of his company “Iron Clad Manufacturing Co.” as his health declines.

1902 – Elizabeth obtains the patents for a milk can and stacking garbage can using the name of “Elizabeth Cochrane Seaman”.

1904 – Elizabeth’s husband Robert dies. “Iron Clad” begins producing the modern 55 gallon oil drum under Elizabeth’s leadership. Unfortunately, Elizabeth is not experienced in the financial aspect of management and the company suffers from embezzlement by an employee. The company goes bankrupt and she becomes a reporter once again.

1913 – Elizabeth writes articles for the New York Evening Journal covering women’s suffrage.

1914 – Elizabeth acts as a war correspondent during World War I. She sends back reports from the Eastern front and is eventually arrested as a spy.

January 27, 1922 – Elizabeth dies from pneumonia at the age of 57 in New York City.

1998 – Elizabeth is posthumously inducted into the “National Women’s Hall of Fame”
